Empresas de colectivos en conflicto
The Argentine government has called a meeting with bus companies for Thursday to discuss a possible solution to the conflict over subsidy updates, aiming to prevent a potential strike and regularize the service, which has been experiencing delays due to a surge in gasoil prices and fuel shortages. The meeting, scheduled for 10:30 am at the Secretariat of Transportation, led by Fernando Herrmann, will focus on updating subsidies, with the current subsidy measured in January at around $1,700 per liter of gasoil. The bus companies have warned that the service could be interrupted on Wednesday.

Gobierno elimina programa Volver al Trabajo
The Argentine government, led by President Javier Milei, officially announced the elimination of the Volver al Trabajo program, which will cease to exist in April. Approximately 900,000 beneficiaries will no longer receive assistance, and instead, will be offered courses of training without payment. The decision affects particularly women who run community kitchens and workers in the informal economy, such as textile workers and recyclers.
